Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Praise Dynasty Real Estate Ltd ("Praise Dynasty", "we", "us", "our"). We understand that confidentiality and discretion are paramount for our high-net-worth (HNI) clients. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information when you visit our website, communicate with us, or utilize our luxury real estate services in Nigeria and globally.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you express an interest in obtaining information about us or our properties, or when you contact us. The personal information we collect may include:

  • Identity Data: Full name, title, date of birth, and government-issued identification (Passports, National IDs).
  • Contact Data: Residential address, email address, and telephone/WhatsApp numbers.
  • Financial Data: Proof of funds, bank references, and source of wealth documentation necessary for high-value transactions.
  • Property Preferences: Criteria for property searches, viewing requests, and investment objectives.

3. Use of Your Information

We use the information we collect or receive to:

  • Facilitate private property viewings and virtual tours.
  • Process real estate transactions and draft legal agreements.
  • Comply with regulatory and legal obligations.
  • Send administrative information to you, such as updates to our terms, conditions, and policies.
  • Deliver targeted communication regarding off-market properties that match your high-level portfolio criteria.

4. Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Compliance

As a luxury real estate agency operating within Nigeria and handling high-value transactions, Praise Dynasty is strictly bound by local and international Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorism Financing (CTF) regulations. We are legally required to conduct Know Your Customer (KYC) checks and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) prior to commencing any transactional relationship. We reserve the right to request comprehensive identity and financial documentation and to retain this data as mandated by law.

5. Cookie Usage

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to access or store information. Cookies are small data files stored on your hard drive or in device memory that help us improve our services and your experience, see which areas and features of our services are popular, and track visits. You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, but this may affect the functionality of our website.

6. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your information with:

  • Legal and financial advisors, notaries, and relevant government agencies to facilitate your transaction.
  • Third-party service providers who perform services for us or on our behalf (e.g., IT hosting, data analysis).
  • Law enforcement or regulatory bodies when required to comply with court orders, laws, or legal processes, including AML audits.

7. Data Security

We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect the security of any personal information we process. However, please also remember that we cannot guarantee that the internet itself is 100% secure. Although we will do our best to protect your personal information, transmission of personal information to and from our website is at your own risk. You should only access the website within a secure environment.
